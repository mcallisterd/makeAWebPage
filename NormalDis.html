Normal Distribution

Back A normal distribution approximates a great variety of natural phenomena, justified via the Central Limit Theorem, which shows that the sum of enough random variables of any distribution tends towards a normal distribution. Heights of animals, then, might be described by some random variable X, and given enough of that animal in your sample, the distribution of the sum of lots of that height random variable X will be normal. In fact, this can happen as soon as the sample is of size 30! In practical terms this means a couple of things. One, the properties of normal distributions allow you to estimate standard deviations of simple phenomena rather easily, as the "empirical rule" for normal distributions, visualized to the right, states most samples fall within two standard deviations of the mean, so given that most males you've seen are between 6'3" and 5'3", say, and if you happened to know the average height was 5'9", you could estimate the standard deviation for human height to be about 3", which is rather close to the actual 2.9". Further, given that most inherent qualities follow a normal distribution, it tells you that exceptional people, say a person over 7' tall, are actually very rare, beyond what your experience tells you.
